How it works

Setup is a conversation.
The rest happens without you.

STEP 01

You hand over the keys.

A 20-minute conversation. Address, family, the dog, the gym schedule, the dishwasher that's been making that noise. Anaise builds a model of your home.

STEP 02

It connects to your life.

Calendar, email, the grocery store, the pediatrician's portal. Anaise reads what already exists — it doesn't make you re-enter your life into a new app.

STEP 03

It starts noticing.

The HVAC filter is 11 months old. You're out of olive oil based on the recipe history. Maya's well-visit is due. Anaise finds the seams and fills them.

STEP 04

It pushes you decisions.

Three vendor quotes with a recommended pick. A grocery order, drafted. A repair, scheduled unless you say no. Not a notification feed — a short list of things one tap away from done.

Why Anaise

A few things we believe.

Most household tools sell you a way to do more. We sell you a way to do less. These are the principles we work from.

01

The list is the problem.

A to-do list moves the work from your head to a screen, but it doesn't do the work. It only makes the work visible. We don't want to make the work more visible. We want it gone.

02

We push decisions, not pings.

Most apps push you information — "3 items low," "filter due," "want to reorder?" — and ask you to decide. We do the opposite. We make the small calls in your style and only contact you when the answer actually needs to be yours.

03

Silence is the default.

No status updates. No "we did a thing" notifications. If a decision is pushed to you, it's because the cost is real or the call is genuinely yours to make. Otherwise, you don't hear from us — and that's the product working.

"

A normal app pushes you tasks. Anaise pushes you decisions — drafted, vetted, and one tap from done.

04

Defaults beat decisions.

Every "would you like to" question is a tiny tax on attention. We learn your preferences early, decide the small things by default, and reserve your inbox for the calls that deserve a yes.

05

Small things, not big.

We stay out of the high-trust corners of your life — money, taxes, kids, gifts. We focus on the boring, low-stakes upkeep that quietly eats your weekends. Filters. Cleanings. Restocks. Repairs.

06

No vendor commissions, ever.

When the plumber pays the platform, the platform stops working for you. Our only revenue is your subscription. The dishwasher repair guy is just the dishwasher repair guy.

Trust

Your home is yours. We just keep it running.

Anaise sits in a privileged position — calendars, email, the credit card on file. We treat that the way we'd want our own home treated. Quietly. Carefully. With everything documented.

01

Read access, not control.

Anaise reads your calendar and inbox to find the seams. It never sends mail as you, never moves money without an approval, never makes a bookable commitment without a yes.

02

Decisions are logged.

Every action — from the bag of carrots to the $400 plumber — leaves a receipt. You can scroll the ledger end-to-end at any time, export it, or hand it to your accountant.

03

No data resale. Ever.

We don't take vendor commissions and we don't sell your behavior. Our only revenue is your subscription. If we ever change that, you'll hear it from us first, in plain language.

04

A clean exit.
